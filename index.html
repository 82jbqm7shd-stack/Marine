Overview

Hopking’s career is documented across a mixed record of broadcast media, maritime and yachting press, and published expedition literature. Published sources support (i) early expedition activity in the 1990s, (ii) polar operations, (iii) later senior command roles within the large-yacht sector, and (iv) participation in public discussion of regulation and operating practice within the industry.[1][8][9]

Early expedition and adventure sailing

In the early 1990s Hopking worked at the Ridgway Adventure School in Scotland. Hopking attributes his work on the yacht English Rose VI as the start of his professional maritime career. The Adventure School later formed the subject of a Channel 4 Cutting Edge documentary (“Exposure”, broadcast 25 January 1993), in which he featured.[2]

During the same period, Hopking was involved in an expedition associated with sea kayaking around Cape Horn alongside John and Rebecca Ridgway. Contemporary UK press coverage exists in regional newspapers, and Hopking also features in later broadcast and published accounts of the expedition, including an ITV film subsequently made available online and Rebecca Ridgway’s book Something Amazing.[3][4][5]

Polar operations and the 2041 programme

Hopking served as captain of the expedition yacht 2041, acquired for operations associated with Antarctic Treaty awareness and environmental programming. Argentine press reporting from October 2000 describes the vessel’s arrival in Buenos Aires and identifies Hopking as the yacht’s captain in connection with the programme’s objectives.[6]

The programme and voyage narrative are also documented in the book 2041: Voyage South, a published account associated with the expedition’s wider public communication.[7]

Superyacht command and operational leadership

By the late 2000s and early 2010s Hopking was operating within the large-yacht sector. A BOAT International charter review published in 2011 identifies him as captain of the motor yacht Laurel.[8]

Subsequent published material credits him as captain of the motor yacht Hampshire II during the period in which his name appears in professional commentary on regulation and operating practice in The Crew Report (SuperyachtNews / The Superyacht Group). [9][15]

During this phase of his career, Hopking is listed by the Global Business Excellence Awards as a previous winner of “Outstanding Personal Achievement” for his work to develop an industry leading Policy and Procedure Management tool which led its field in document control systems.[10]

Governance, compliance and documentation disciplines

Published sources attribute to Hopking participation in industry discussion concerning regulatory load and operational practice. A winter 2015 issue of The Crew Report includes a contribution credited to “Captain Mark Hopking, M/Y Hampshire II” in a feature framed around regulatory pressures and code compliance.[9]

While such commentary is not itself a formal credential, it is relevant as public evidence of involvement in professional debate around governance, compliance and the practical implications of codes and inspections in large-yacht operations.[9]

New-build delivery: Sherpa (Feadship)

Hopking later served in senior delivery roles on the Feadship explorer yacht Sherpa. Trade press reporting on Sherpa’s launch and build appears in SuperyachtNews and other specialist outlets, providing independent documentation of the project and timeframe.[11]

A separate documentary on YouTube (often circulated under the title “The Making of a Dream”) presents a behind-the-scenes account of the build; it is included here as a broadcast-style source for the project’s public record.[12]
