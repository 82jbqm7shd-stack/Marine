Maritime career

A research-led account of the maritime career of Mark Richard Hopking, based on published sources and industry coverage.

Methodology and scope

This page distinguishes between information supported by published sources and material held in private records. Where evidence is unpublished or privately held, it is treated conservatively and not presented as independently verifiable.

Overview

Hopking’s professional career developed from expedition and adventure-based maritime operations in the early 1990s into senior command and project leadership roles within the superyacht sector. His work has been documented through a combination of contemporaneous press reporting, broadcast documentaries, specialist industry publications, and regulatory records.

Early expedition and adventure sailing

In the early 1990s, Hopking was associated with expedition and adventure-based maritime activity connected with the John Ridgway Adventure School in Sutherland, Scotland. During this period he participated in a sea-kayaking expedition around Cape Horn, an undertaking documented in contemporaneous press coverage and later broadcast media.

The expedition featured in a 1993 ITV documentary and in an episode of Channel 4’s Cutting Edge documentary strand, and was also described in published narrative accounts of the expedition.

Antarctic operations

In 1999, Hopking served as captain of the yacht 2041, acquired by environmentalist Robert Swan as part of an Antarctic Treaty awareness programme. In this role, he skippered the vessel from the United Kingdom to the Antarctic Peninsula, supporting expeditionary activity and public engagement around polar environmental protection.

The voyage and associated expeditions were documented in published form, contributing to the public record of the programme.

Superyacht command

From the early 2000s, Hopking’s career focused increasingly on large private motor yachts. Between 2011 and 2016, he commanded the 78-metre motor yacht Hampshire II. During this period, he contributed to published discussion within the superyacht industry concerning regulatory change, professional standards, and operational practice.

New build and project leadership

Hopking later acted as build captain and project leader for the 74-metre Feadship explorer yacht Sherpa, overseeing construction at the Royal Van Lent shipyard. The build process attracted coverage in specialist yachting media and was followed in a documentary film that examined the design, construction, and operational intent of the vessel.

In recognition of associated professional practice and project delivery, Hopking received a Global Business Excellence Award.

Professional credentials

Hopking holds a Master’s Certificate of Competency issued by the UK Maritime & Coastguard Agency, which can be verified through the MCA’s online certificate validation service. His professional standing is further reflected in published affiliations and industry recognition.
