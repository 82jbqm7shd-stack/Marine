Maritime career

A research-led account of the maritime career of Mark Richard Hopking, based on published sources and industry coverage.

Related pages: Heraldry · Barony of Balvill · Genealogy

Methodology and scope

This page distinguishes between information supported by published sources and material held in private records. Where evidence is unpublished or privately held, it is treated conservatively and not presented as independently verifiable.

Overview

Hopking’s professional career developed from expedition and adventure-based maritime operations in the early 1990s into senior command and project leadership roles within the superyacht sector. His work has been documented through a combination of contemporaneous press reporting, broadcast documentaries, specialist industry publications, and regulatory records.

Early expedition and adventure sailing

In the early 1990s, Hopking undertook expedition and adventure-based maritime work connected with John and Rebecca Ridgway. He participated in a sea-kayaking expedition around Cape Horn that was documented in contemporaneous press coverage and later media sources, including a 1993 ITV film and published narrative accounts of the expedition.[2][3][4][5]

Separately, Hopking’s work at the Ridgway Adventure School in Sutherland, Scotland, formed the subject of a Channel 4 Cutting Edge documentary episode concerned with management training and programme delivery at the school.[1][6]

Antarctic operations

In 1999, Hopking served as captain of the yacht 2041, acquired by environmentalist Robert Swan as part of an Antarctic Treaty awareness programme. In this role, he skippered the vessel from the United Kingdom to the Antarctic Peninsula, supporting expeditionary activity and public engagement around polar environmental protection.[7][8]

Superyacht command

From the early 2000s, Hopking’s career focused increasingly on large private motor yachts. Between 2011 and 2016, he commanded the 78-metre motor yacht Hampshire II and contributed to published industry discussion concerning regulatory pressures and change within the sector.[9][10]

During this period, he received recognition through the Global Business Excellence Awards.[11]

New build and project leadership

Hopking later acted as build captain and project manager for the 74-metre Feadship explorer yacht Sherpa, overseeing construction at Feadship’s Royal Van Lent shipyard. The build and launch attracted coverage in specialist yachting media, and Hopking featured in documentary material relating to the project.[12][13][15]

Professional credentials

Hopking holds a Master’s Certificate of Competency issued by the UK Maritime & Coastguard Agency, which can be verified through the MCA’s online certificate validation service.[14]
