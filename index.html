Hopking’s career is documented across a mixed record of broadcast media, maritime and yachting press, and published expedition literature. Published sources support:

Early expedition and adventure sailing

In the early 1990s Hopking worked at the Ridgway Adventure School in Scotland. Hopking attributes his work on the yacht English Rose VI during this period as the start of his professional maritime career. The Adventure School later formed the subject of a Channel 4 Cutting Edge documentary (“Exposure”, broadcast 25 January 1993), in which he featured.[2]

During the same period, Hopking was involved in an expedition, sea kayaking around Cape Horn alongside John and Rebecca Ridgway. Contemporary UK press coverage exists in regional newspapers[3]. Hopking features in broadcast and published accounts of the expedition, including an ITV film and Rebecca Ridgway’s book Something Amazing [4][5]

Polar operations and the 2041 programme

Hopking served as captain of the expedition yacht 2041, acquired for operations associated with Antarctic Treaty awareness and environmental conservation. Argentine press reporting from October 2000 describes the vessel’s mission and identifies Hopking as the yacht’s captain in connection with the programme’s objectives.[6] The programme and voyage narrative are documented in the book 2041: Voyage South.[7]

Superyacht command and operational leadership

By the late 2000s and early 2010s Hopking was operating within the large-yacht sector. A BOAT International charter review published in 2011 identifies him as captain of the motor yacht Laurel.[8]

Subsequent published material credits him as captain of the motor yacht Hampshire II during the period in which his name appears in professional commentary on regulation and operating practice in The Crew Report (SuperyachtNews / The Superyacht Group). [9][14]

During this phase of his career, Hopking is listed by the Global Business Excellence Awards as a previous winner of “Outstanding Personal Achievement” for his work to develop an industry leading Policy and Procedure Management tool which led its field in document control systems.[10]

Governance, compliance and documentation disciplines

Published sources attribute to Hopking's participation in industry discussion concerning regulatory load and operational practice. A winter 2015 issue of The Crew Report includes a contribution credited to "Captain Mark Hopking of M/Y Hampshire II" in a feature framed around regulatory pressures and code compliance.[9] Hopking's practical insight and opinion within the debate around governance and compliance is sort by professionals and authorities alike.

New-build project delivery

Hopking later served in a senior project delivery role during the design and construction of the Feadship explorer yacht Sherpa. Trade press reporting on Sherpa’s launch and build appears in SuperyachtNews and other specialist outlets.[11] A separate documentary presents a behind-the-scenes account of the build.[12]
